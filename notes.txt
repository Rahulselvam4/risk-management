Because this is a financial risk model, standard Accuracy is a dangerous metric. 
I focused on the Confusion Matrix, specifically prioritizing Recall over Precision. 
Missing a market crash (a False Negative) destroys a user's portfolio, 
whereas falsely predicting a crash (a False Positive) just means the user plays it safe for a day. 
Therefore, I optimized the model's threshold to maximize Recall, 
ensuring we catch as many true high-risk days as possible, 
while using the SHAP waterfall charts to let the human user verify the 'False Positives'.


True Positive (TP): Model predicted Risk, and the market crashed. (Success)

True Negative (TN): Model predicted Safe, and the market was fine. (Success)

False Positive (FP): Model predicted Risk, but the market was fine. (False Alarm)

False Negative (FN): Model predicted Safe, but the market crashed! (Disaster)
